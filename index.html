Here's a time-line of Bill Gates's life


  • 1955 - October 28: William Henry Gates III is born in Seattle, Washington, to William H. Gates Sr. and Mary Maxwell Gates.

  • 1968 - Gates begins programming at Lakeside School, creating a tic-tac-toe game and showing early signs of his interest in computers.

  • 1973 - Gates enters Harvard University but drops out in 1975 to co-found Microsoft with Paul Allen, focusing on software development.

  • 1975 - Microsoft is founded, and Gates and Allen develop a version of the BASIC programming language for the Altair 8800 microcomputer.

  • 1980 - Microsoft signs a crucial deal with IBM to provide an operating system (OS) for their PC. Gates retains the rights to license MS-DOS to other manufacturers.

  • 1985 - Microsoft Windows 1.0 is released, marking the beginning of the Windows OS series, providing a graphical user interface for PCs.

  • 1990 - Microsoft releases Windows 3.0, a highly successful version that contributes to the widespread adoption of Microsoft operating systems.

  • 1995 - Windows 95 is launched, introducing significant improvements and features, becoming a major success for Microsoft.

  • 2000 - Gates steps down as CEO of Microsoft but continues to be actively involved, focusing on software architecture and new technologies.

  • 2008 - Gates transitions away from day-to-day operations at Microsoft to dedicate more time to philanthropy through the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation.

  • 2014 - Satya Nadella becomes CEO of Microsoft, and Gates remains involved as a technology advisor, providing guidance on key projects.

  • 2021 - Bill and Melinda Gates announce their divorce, but they continue their collaboration on the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, focusing on global health and education.

  • Present - Bill Gates continues to be a prominent figure in technology and philanthropy, addressing global challenges such as health, education, and climate change.
